About

“What excites me most is the bridge between theory and the bench — where gene expression, protein function and the logic of evolution stop being diagrams and start being data.”

I'm a BSc Biological Sciences student at the University of Reading, focused on molecular biology, biotechnology and genetics. Weekly practicals are where I'm happiest: pipetting, microscopy, and learning to record and interpret results carefully and honestly.

Outside the lab I lead as President of the Surf Society (26/27), volunteered with Cancer Research UK — running 100 miles in a month as part of a team that raised over £4,000 — and hold the Gold Duke of Edinburgh's Award.

Part-time, I cook Filipino street food at Kanto: high pressure, tight timing, exacting hygiene. It turns out a busy kitchen and a busy lab reward exactly the same habits.
