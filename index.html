🧬 About Me

Driven by curiosity, grounded in science

I'm a BSc Biological Sciences student at the University of Reading, currently in my first year with a strong focus on molecular biology, biotechnology, and genetics. I thrive when bridging theory and practice — whether in the lab, through project work, or via hands-on roles in research-adjacent environments.

What drives me is the fascination with how life works at the molecular level: gene expression, protein function, and the elegant logic of evolution. I'm keen on developing both laboratory and research skills — from practical pipetting and microscopy to data analysis and rigorous scientific communication. Beyond coursework, I volunteer for causes that matter: I've worked with Cancer Research UK and held leadership as President of the Surf Society, balancing science with wider student experiences.

I'm also gaining real-world experience outside university: I work as a chef at a local restaurant, which has sharpened my attention to detail, time management under pressure, and teamwork — all transferable to a lab environment.
